Help

  • Tilting the globe
    • Tilt the globe by dragging with the mouse or your finger.
  • Setting it spinning
    • You can set the globe rotating clockwise by clicking/ tapping in the lower left corner outside of the globe but within a square circumscribing it. For counter-clockwise rotation, use the lower right corner. The upper right corner will stop the rotation while the upper left resets it to the home location (latitude 0, longitude 0 at the center).
  • Picking the origin and destination
    • You can launch a balloon whenever you have latitudes and longitudes showing for the origin and destination. The lighted triangle shows which will be filled in if you click on the globe. You can click on a triangle to choose that item. If you click on an entry box, it will clear that and prepare for a new entry. Typing the first few letters will generally give you a set of options; you can click on an option. When there is only one choice, it will be filled in (green coloring). Clicking on the box allows you to start over. Tab moves from box to box.
  • Setting the flight height
    • On the height scale to the right of the globe, drag the balloon image up or down. The display changes to show 12 hr trajectories at that height starting at the red dots.
  • Day/night vertical levels
    • Solar balloons gain altitude during the day and lose it during the night, since the infrared coming from the surface does not heat it as effectively. Clouds can also be a concern. In the trajectory computations, we have simply let the balloon fall to the next lower height at night and rise to to the height on the balloon slider during the day. Switch modes by clicking on the icon to the right of the timeline, below the height slider.

Resources

  • Data source for wind forecast
    • The Global Forecast System produces current and forecast winds for 16 days ahead, covering the whole atmosphere. The data includes many other meteorological fields, as well. We use the most recent midnight or noon UTC data.
